"Ground floor" -> § 11-3101(a) -> Visible from the street and directly accessible to the public from the street.
"Ground floor commercial premises" -> § 11-3101(a) -> Any ground floor premises that is occupied or used, or could be occupied or used, for the purpose of offering or selling goods at retail.
"Lease" -> § 11-3101(a) -> A lease or a rental agreement, license agreement or month to month tenancy.
"Occupied" -> § 11-3101(a) -> In use.
"Rent" -> § 11-3101(a) -> Has the same meaning as set forth in section 7-01 of title 19 of the rules of the city of New York.
"Second floor" -> § 11-3101(a) -> The second floor of a building, visible from the street, and accessible to the public directly from the street or from the interior of a building.
"Second floor commercial premises" -> § 11-3101(a) -> Any second floor premises that is occupied or used, or could be occupied or used, for the purpose of offering of selling goods at retail.
"Tenant" -> § 11-3101(a) -> Has the same meaning as set forth in section 7-01 of title 19 of the rules of the city of New York.
"Vacancy information" -> § 11-3101(a) -> (i) A statement that the premises was vacant on either June 30 or December 31 of the current calendar year; and, (ii) the expiration date of the most recent lease for such premises, if any, whose start date preceded the reported vacancy date.
"Vacant" -> § 11-3101(a) -> Not occupied by a commercial tenant pursuant to a lease, excluding for purposes of construction or renovation.
"Person" -> § 11-4001(a) -> Includes, but shall not be limited to, an individual, corporation (including a dissolved corporation), partnership, association, trust or estate.
"Person" -> § 11-4001(b) -> Also includes an officer, employee or agent of a corporation; a member, employee or agent of a partnership or association; an employee or agent of an individual proprietorship; an employee or agent of an estate or trust; or a fiduciary.
"Felony" and "misdemeanor" -> § 11-4001(c) -> Have the same meaning as in the penal law, with exceptions regarding fines.
"City" -> § 11-4001(d)(1) -> The city of New York.
"State" -> § 11-4001(d)(2) -> The state of New York.
"Tax fraud act" -> § 11-4002(a) -> Willfully engaging in or causing another to engage in specified acts, including failure to file returns, filing false returns, supplying false information, engaging in schemes to defraud, failing to remit collected tax, failing to collect required tax, failing to pay tax with intent to evade, or issuing false exemption certificates.

[PROCEDURES / ENFORCEMENT]
Mailing rules -> § 11-1933(a) -> If a claim or document is mailed, the postmark date is deemed the date of delivery. Registered mail registration is prima facie evidence of delivery.
Collection procedures (Earnings Tax) -> § 11-1934(a) -> Taxes are collected by the commissioner, who establishes the mode or time for collection.
Warrants for collection -> § 11-1934(c) -> If tax is not paid within ten days after notice and demand, the commissioner may issue a warrant to the sheriff or department employee to levy upon and sell the person's property.
Transferee liability -> § 11-1935(a) -> Liability of a transferee of property of a taxpayer is assessed, paid, and collected in the same manner as the tax to which the liability relates, with certain limitations extensions.
Bulk sales notification -> § 11-1936 -> Purchaser of bulk merchandise/fixtures must notify the commissioner at least ten days before taking possession or paying; failure to notify results in a first priority lien for taxes due from the seller and personal liability for the purchaser.
Jeopardy assessment -> § 11-1937(a) -> If the commissioner believes collection will be jeopardized by delay, they shall immediately assess and proceed to collect the deficiency.
Criminal penalties (Earnings Tax) -> § 11-1938 -> Defines misdemeanors for attempts to evade tax, failure to file/pay, or supplying false info. Sets fines and imprisonment terms.
Secrecy of returns -> § 11-1942 -> Unlawful to divulge information from reports or returns except in accordance with judicial order or law; violations punishable by fine, imprisonment, and dismissal from office.
Sales Tax on Credit Services Imposition -> § 11-2040 -> Imposes a 4.5% tax on receipts from credit rating and reporting services.
Parking Tax Imposition -> § 11-2049 -> Imposes an 8% tax on receipts from providing parking, garaging, or storing motor vehicles in certain areas, with exemptions for homeowner associations.
Parking Tax Penalties -> § 11-2052(c) -> Penalties for wrongful use of exemption certificates: $100 per violation for negligence, $500 for fraud.
Liquor/Beer Tax Imposition -> § 11-2056 -> Imposes excise taxes on distributors and noncommercial importers of beer (12 cents/gallon) and liquors (26.4 cents/liter).
Real Property Transfer Tax Imposition -> § 11-2102 -> Imposes tax on deeds and transfers of economic interests in real property where consideration exceeds $25,000, with varying rates based on property type and consideration amount.
Presumption of taxability (RPTT) -> § 11-2103 -> Presumes all deeds and transfers are taxable until the contrary is established; burden of proof is on the taxpayer.
RPTT Payment -> § 11-2104 -> Tax payable by grantor to commissioner within 30 days of delivery of deed/transfer; grantee liable if grantor fails to pay.
RPTT Returns -> § 11-2105 -> Joint return filed by grantor and grantee within 30 days of delivery/transfer. Electronic filing may be required.
Determination of RPTT -> § 11-2107 -> If return not filed or incorrect, commissioner determines tax; determination final unless petition for hearing filed within 90 days.
RPTT Refunds -> § 11-2108 -> Commissioner refunds erroneously collected tax if application made within one year of payment.
Proceedings to recover RPTT -> § 11-2111 -> Corporation counsel brings action to enforce payment; commissioner may issue warrant to sheriff to levy upon property.
RPTT Interest -> § 11-2114(a) -> Interest on underpayments at rate set by commissioner (default 7.5%) compounded daily.
RPTT Penalties -> § 11-2114(b)-(e) -> Additions for failure to file (5-25%), failure to pay (0.5-25%), negligence (5% + 50% of interest), fraud (50% + 50% of interest).
Motor Vehicle Tax Payment -> § 11-2204 -> Tax paid to commissioner of motor vehicles upon registration/renewal; payment is condition precedent to registration.
Motor Vehicle Tax Penalties -> § 11-2214(a) -> Penalty of 5 times the tax plus 5% interest per month of delay for failure to file/pay.
E911 Surcharge Collection -> § 11-2325 -> Service suppliers act as collection agents, remit funds monthly, retain 2% admin fee.
Wireless Surcharge Collection -> § 11-2344 -> Wireless service suppliers collect surcharge, remit monthly, retain 2% admin fee.
Liquor Licensee Tax Returns -> § 11-2405 -> Returns filed by June 25th each tax year.
Liquor Licensee Tax Payment -> § 11-2406 -> Tax due on the last day return is required to be filed.
Hotel Room Tax Imposition -> § 11-2502 -> Imposes tax on hotel room occupancy at specified rates plus percentage of rent.
Hotel Tax Registration -> § 11-2514 -> Operators/room remarketers must file certificate of registration within 3 days of commencing business; certificate of authority to collect tax issued.
Hotel Tax Penalties (Records) -> § 11-2515(i)-(k) -> Penalties for failure to maintain/make available records (up to $1,000-$5,000 per quarter).
Mortgage Tax Imposition -> § 11-2601 -> Imposes tax on recording of mortgages on real property within the city, rates based on property type and amount.
Mortgage Tax Payment -> § 11-2602 -> Payable to recording officer of the county upon recording.
Vault Charge Imposition -> § 11-2702 -> Annual charge for privilege of occupying/using/maintaining a vault in city streets.
Vault Charge Returns -> § 11-2704 -> Filed by July 15th annually showing dimensions.
Fire Insurance Proceeds Claim -> § 11-2801(3) -> Commissioner claims special lien against fire insurance proceeds for unpaid taxes/charges; lien is prior to other claims except mortgagee of record.
Sustainable Energy Loans -> § 11-3003 -> Program makes loans to property owners for renewable energy/efficiency improvements.
Loan Repayment (Sustainable Energy) -> § 11-3005 -> Loan constitutes a lien on real property; repaid through charge on real property collected like municipal taxes.
Commercial Premises Registration -> § 11-3101(b)-(d) -> Owners of ground/second floor commercial premises must register annually and report vacancy information.
Commercial Premises Registry Penalty -> § 11-3101(g) -> Failure to file constitutes failure to file income/expense statement, subject to penalties under § 11-208.1.
City criminal tax fraud -> § 11-4003 to 11-4007 -> Defines degrees of criminal tax fraud (5th through 1st) based on amount of tax evaded and timeframe; classifies as misdemeanor or felony.
Failure to obey subpoenas -> § 11-4010 -> Misdemeanor for failure to attend, refuse to be sworn/answer, or produce documents.
Cigarette tax evasion -> § 11-4012 -> Defines felonies and misdemeanors for evasion of cigarette tax, possession of unstamped cigarettes, and counterfeiting stamps.
Seizure of cigarettes -> § 11-4021 -> Police/peace officers authorized to seize unstamped cigarettes; subject to forfeiture and sale/destruction.
Authority to seal premises -> § 11-4023 -> Commissioner authorized to seal premises and remove/seal devices for repeat violations of cigarette/tobacco tax laws.
Seizure of untaxed tobacco products -> § 11-4025 -> Authorization to seize and forfeit untaxed tobacco products and vending machines.
